1976: Founding of Apple

1976

Apple was founded on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in Cupertino, California. They released the Apple I computer, which was the company's first product.

1984: Introduction of Macintosh

1984

Apple introduced the Macintosh computer on January 24, 1984. It featured a graphical user interface and was marketed with the famous "1984" commercial during the Super Bowl.

1997: Return of Steve Jobs

1997

Steve Jobs returned to Apple after being ousted in 1985. Apple was facing financial difficulties at the time. Jobs took over as interim CEO and began a period of restructuring and innovation.

2001: Launch of iPod

2001

On October 23, 2001, Apple introduced the iPod, a portable digital music player. The iPod became immensely popular and marked Apple's entry into the consumer electronics market.

2007: Introduction of iPhone

2007

On June 29, 2007, Apple revolutionized the smartphone industry by releasing the first iPhone. The device combined a phone, iPod, and internet communicator, changing the way people interacted with technology.

2010: Launch of iPad

2010

Apple launched the iPad on April 3, 2010, creating a new market for tablet computers. The device became a commercial success and further solidified Apple's influence in the consumer electronics industry.
